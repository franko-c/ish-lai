Images

There are so many image formats available - though some in particular, are really good for using on the web. What are they?

JPGs

The JPG format is useful for when you want to show off a high quality image, or one with a large colour palette, whilst keeping the size down. JPGs don't allow for transparent pixels. They're great for photos and images with gradients in them.

GIFs

GIFs are the washed up celebrities of internet image formats...they are 'the go-to option for simple animations...' and support transparency as a binary option - that means they are either see-through, or not. Don't use them for photos!

Once upon a time, they were everywhere and used by everyone; Facebook posts and comments, in e-mails, on forums and to this day there exists a Reddit community dedicated to producing only the highest quality animated images.

PNGs

PNGs are great for anything that's not a photo or animated. For photos, a PNG file of the same quality (as perceived the human eye) would generally be bigger than an equivalent JPG file.

However, they do deal with opacity just fine, and they don't have color palette limitations. This makes them an excellent fit for icons, technical diagrams, logos, etc.

SVGs

SVGs are genuinely awesome in that they are vector based - which means they can be scaled up or down to any size and they'll look just as good, without loss of quality!.

This property makes SVGs awesome for responsive design., as well as for icons, logos and pretty much anything that's not a photo. If you can get your image as an SVG - use it!

In my experience, they're not as popular as the other formats listed, although they are infinitely more useful, due to the mobile-first approach that we're seeing grow in popularity.
